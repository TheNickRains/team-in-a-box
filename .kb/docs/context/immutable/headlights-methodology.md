# Headlights Methodology

Only plan the next 6ft. You can only see as far as your headlights — plan to that distance, no further.

## The rule

Add abstractions, libraries, personas, and helper modules when the task demands them — not before. Vision can be clear; execution is just-in-time. The shape of step 4 depends on what step 1 returns. You don't know that yet, so don't pretend you do.

## In practice

- Create the next one or two dispatchable tasks. When they return, create the next.
- The backlog is a direction, not a commitment.
- A wrong dispatch wastes a full agent spawn on the wrong thing. A one-round boardroom is cheaper than rework.
- When too much is in flight or the next step is unclear, call General. One verdict, one load-bearing reason.

## The test

Before adding any library, abstraction, helper module, or "while we're here" refactor — ask: does the task in front of me right now require this? If no, stop.

## General is the enforcer

General's job is to hold this line. One verdict, one load-bearing reason. No speculative open issues, no pre-sequenced next moves. General never caves to scope creep.
